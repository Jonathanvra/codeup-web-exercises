National Parks

How many areas are in the National Park System?
The system includes 423 areas covering more than 85 million acres in every state, the District of Columbia, American Samoa, Guam, Puerto Rico, and the Virgin Islands. These areas include national parks, monuments, battlefields, military parks, historical parks, historic sites, lakeshores, seashores, recreation areas, scenic rivers and trails, and the White House.
How many people visit the national parks?
Total recreation visitors to the national parks in 2021: 297,115,406.
What is the most-visited national park?
Blue Ridge Parkway at 15.9 Million visitors.
What is the largest national park site?
Wrangell-St. Elias National Park and Preserve, AK, at 13.2 million acres.
What is the smallest national park site?
Thaddeus Kosciuszko National Memorial, PA, at 0.02 acres.
Can I bring my pet to a national park?
Some national parks welcome pets—in developed areas, on many trails and campgrounds, and in some lodging facilities.
How old is the National Park System?
The National Park Service was created by an act signed by President Woodrow Wilson on August 25, 1916. Yellowstone National Park was established by an act signed by President Ulysses S. Grant on March 1, 1872, as the nation's first national park.
Who is the director of the National Park Service?
Charles F. "Chuck" Sams III is the Director of the National Park Service.
What government agency oversees the National Park Service?
The National Park Service is a bureau of the Department of the Interior. Directly overseeing its operation is the department's Assistant Secretary for Fish, Wildlife and Parks.
What year has had the most recreational visitors?
The year with the most National park visitors is 2016 with 330,971,689 visitors.

Guadalupe Mountains

  • Guadalupe Mountains covers a total area of 86,416 acres or 135 square miles.
  • Guadalupe Mountains was made a national park on October 15, 1966.
  • Guadalupe Mountains’ lowest point is 3,636 feet near Williams Road.
  • The highest point in Guadalupe Mountains is 8,751 feet on Guadalupe Peak.

Big Bend

  • Big Bend covers a total area of 801,163 acres or 1,252 square miles.
  • Big Bend was made a national park on June 20, 1935.
  • Big Bend’s lowest point is 1,715 feet at Rio Grande River.
  • The highest point in Big Bend is 7,832 feet on Emory Peak.

Mesa Verde

  • Mesa Verde covers a total area of 52,074 acres or 81 square miles.
  • Mesa Verde was made a national park on June 29, 1906.
  • Mesa Verde’s lowest point is 6,015 feet in Soda Canyon.
  • The highest point in Mesa Verde is 8,571 feet at Park Point.
